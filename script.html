Presentation Script · April 13, 2026 · For Olga Kornyushyna

37 Practices of a Bodhisattva

Sangha Study Groups and English Book Publication · 4 minutes spoken

Opening (30 seconds)

Thank you for this opportunity to share something that has become very dear to my heart.

Many of you know the 37 Practices of a Bodhisattva by Gyalse Tokme Zangpo. Garchen Rinpoche has called it the heart of his teaching and recommends it to all his students.

"If you follow this text by the letter, you will overcome your suffering."

Today I want to tell you about a project that grew from those words.

The Project (1 minute)

We are building two things together.

First: peer-led sangha study groups around the world, supported by a structured toolkit so that any community can study this text together. A group in Singapore has already begun forming. Groups are starting in the United States. The toolkit provides session structure, opening practices, facilitator guidance, and a way to ground each gathering in lived experience rather than abstraction.

Second: the English translation of my commentary on this text. I wrote this book in Ukrainian. I began it before the Russian invasion and completed it during the war's opening months. It has accompanied readers in bomb shelters, in occupied territories, and in exile. It was recommended alongside Viktor Frankl as one of the most useful mental health resources during the conflict.

The Book (1 minute)

The commentary includes over 40 original mindfulness exercises, one for each chapter, tested during the most difficult circumstances I have known. It is science-informed. It draws on contemporary research alongside the classical teachings.

Garchen Rinpoche has personally expressed his wish for this book to reach English readers. Before I left Arizona in January and the winter retreat, he presented me with a vajra and bell and told me he wants me to teach.

· · ·

Translation begins in May, with a professional human translator. We expect to complete it by the end of summer.

Fundraising (45 seconds)

We are raising $15,000 to cover translation and publication costs. All donations go through the Garchen Buddhist Institute, a 501(c)(3), so they are fully tax-deductible. You can give directly through the GBI website.

I am grateful to share that we have already received $1,100 in pledges in the first days. Our first donation of $100 arrived almost immediately. A dharma sister has pledged $1,000. The sangha's generosity is already evident.

How to Participate (30 seconds)

If you feel moved by this, there are several ways to help.

You can start or join a study group in your community. You can support the book fund through GBI. You can share this with your sangha network. And once groups are running, I will offer weekly live Q&A sessions, rotating across time zones, so every community has access.

Closing (15 seconds)

The mission is simple. Not study as an intellectual exercise, but study as refuge. As a direct response to what is actually happening in your life right now.

Thank you. If you are interested, please speak with me or with Brian and Ryan after this session.
